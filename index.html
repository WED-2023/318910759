    • About Albert Einstein

    Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Germany.

    Six weeks later, his family moved to Munich, where his father, Hermann Einstein, and uncle founded an electrical engineering company.

    From a young age, Albert displayed a profound curiosity and aptitude for mathematics and physics.

    Despite facing challenges in securing an academic position after graduating from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic School in Zurich, he eventually took a job at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern in 1902.

    Annus Mirabilis - The Miracle Year:

    In 1905, often referred to as his "miracle year," Einstein published four groundbreaking papers that would transform the landscape of physics:

    1. Photoelectric Effect: Light consists of particles (quanta), explaining how electrons are emitted when exposed to light. Won him the Nobel Prize in 1921.
    2. Brownian Motion: Explained the random movement of particles in fluid, proving the existence of atoms.
    3. Special Theory of Relativity: Time and space are relative and form a four-dimensional spacetime.
    4. Mass-Energy Equivalence: Famous equation E=mc¬≤, showing mass and energy are interchangeable.

    He moved to the U.S. in 1933 due to the Nazi rise, joining the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. He became a citizen in 1940 and supported civil rights, Zionism, and peace. He passed away in 1955.

    Hobbies

    Einstein loved music and played the violin from age five. He admired composers like Mozart and Bach, saying, "Life without playing music is inconceivable for me." Music fueled his creativity.

    He also enjoyed sailing (despite not knowing how to swim), walking, reading philosophy, and daydreaming.

    In Popular Culture

    Einstein became a global symbol of genius and creativity. His image, especially the one with his tongue out, appears in memes, posters, and pop culture.

    Q&A


    Question 1: What are three main differences between Web1, Web2, and Web3?

    Aspect Web1 Web2 Web3
    Content and Interaction Read-only. Static pages, no user input. Read & Write. Users interact and create content (e.g., social media). Ownership. Users own data and assets via blockchain.
    Data Ownership Website-controlled content and data. Platforms collect and control user data. Users control data via decentralized identity and wallets.
    Technology Static HTML, basic websites. JavaScript, APIs, cloud-based services. Blockchain, smart contracts, decentralized apps (DApps).


    Question 2: Show the differences from Question 1 using a use case. For example, a search query like "best car models":

    Aspect Web1 Web2 Web3
    User Experience Static list of top car models on a fixed HTML page. Google returns dynamic search results tailored to your profile. Decentralized query with results curated by community or DAO.
    Interaction No ability to comment or vote. Users can comment, like, share or review models. Users vote on results or earn tokens for contributing reviews.
    Privacy & Control No tracking or personalization. Personal data used by companies to target content. User keeps full control, search is anonymous or wallet-based.
